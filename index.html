📖 About This Project

🎯 Research Objective

This project develops a clinical decision support system for predicting 90-day Major Adverse Cardiac Events (MACE) risk in patients after Percutaneous Coronary Intervention (PCI).

By analyzing 8 routine clinical indicators, the system provides real-time risk assessment to assist clinical decision-making.

📊 Study Design

  • Training Set: 799 patients (MACE+ 85, 10.6%)
  • Testing Set: 343 patients (MACE+ 37, 10.8%)
  • External Validation: 338 patients (MACE+ 37, 10.9%)
  • Features: 8 clinical variables (Age, Sex, BMI, eGFR, Glucose, Hb, RDW, WBC)
  • Methods: 5-fold cross-validation, hyperparameter tuning, SMOTE, threshold optimization

🏆 Key Findings

  • Optimal Model: Naive Bayes (NB)
  • Selection Criteria: Highest External AUROC + Smallest Generalization Gap
  • Test AUROC: 0.841 (0.778-0.899)
  • External AUROC: 0.755 (0.670-0.833)
  • Recommended Threshold: 0.075
  • Key Predictors: Glucose, eGFR, WBC, BMI, Hb

⚕️ Clinical Application

Risk Stratification:

  • 🔴 High Risk (≥0.075): Enhanced monitoring and aggressive intervention recommended
  • 🟡 Moderate Risk (0.05-0.075): Standard care with close follow-up
  • 🟢 Low Risk (<0.05): Routine management
